The HIPAA Privacy Rule defines two paths to de-identification: **Expert Determination** and **Safe Harbor**. Masker is built around Safe Harbor — it's the more prescriptive of the two and the one that can be audited from logs.

Safe Harbor enumerates **18 identifier categories** (45 CFR §164.514(b)(2)). To qualify as de-identified under Safe Harbor, all 18 must be removed.

This page is our honest current coverage matrix.

## What "full" means

A category is **full** when:

- All common shapes are matched by Pass 1 regex with high precision
- All conversational forms are caught by Pass 2 NER with confidence > 0.6
- We've validated against at least two of: i2b2/n2c2, MedDialog, Switchboard, our own benchmark set
- Recall on our internal eval set is ≥ 95%

## What "partial" means

A category is **partial** when:

- Common shapes work
- Long-tail or unusual formats need policy customization (custom regex per tenant)
- Recall on our internal eval is between 70% and 95%

## What "in progress" means

We've scoped the work, the eval set exists, and a target shipping date is in our internal tracker. The May 30, 2026 production launch is the date we're committing to for items L, M, N, P, R reaching at least "partial."

## Why we're being this specific

Most privacy products in this space claim full Safe Harbor coverage. Many of them fail honest red-team tests. We'd rather lose the deal where the prospect needs a category we don't yet cover than win it on a claim we can't defend in an audit.

If your workload depends on a category we mark "partial" or "in progress," tell us. We can either:

1. Accelerate that category in the roadmap (we have done this for pilot customers), or
2. Help you scope a custom policy that hardens the partial coverage with tenant-specific regex, or
3. Recommend you wait until we ship the missing piece.

## How coverage shows up in audit reports

Every compliance report includes the coverage matrix at the time the report was generated. The matrix is **not** static — when you upgrade Masker and we promote a category from partial to full, your next report shows the better coverage automatically.

## Expert Determination

A small number of customers prefer Expert Determination over Safe Harbor (typically because they want to retain some categories for clinical utility). Masker supports this: configure the entity actions you want as `passthrough`, document the determination separately, and the audit log captures the active policy at the time of each call.

We don't provide expert determination services ourselves — that's a relationship between you and a qualified statistician.
